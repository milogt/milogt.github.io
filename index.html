Project

Wumpus World Game Simulation

The Wumpus world is an early computer game, which was originally created by Gregory Yob in 1972. The original version, called Hunt the Wumpus, is based on a simple hide and seek mode that requires players to discover the room with gold in a world with a dangerous monster and multiple obstacles. According to the hints gained while exploring the environment, players need to sense the location of gold and avoid the danger in the meanwhile. In this project, I explored the application of propositional logic in Artificial Intelligence by implementing truth table based entailment.

In order to evaluate the performance of my model, I create a log file to record each round’s result, either win or die, and the path the agent has passed through. After 100 runs, the log file will also record the success rate and the steps taken in average.

Movie Recommendation System

My motivation is to build a movie recommendation engine that can provide personalized recommendation. It is supposed to serve three main customer groups: existing customers, new customers, and independent movie lovers. The datasets I used come from Netflix prize data and TMDB 5000 dataset. The baseline model I use for the existing customers is collaborative filtering, which is a very popular method for recommendation system. Then I combine it with clustering algorithm to achieve other customer recommendation. Depending on the new strategy I invented, the recommendation system becomes more versatile and capable of handling diversity.

For new customers, I trained a clustering model that divided movies into groups, it based on similarities of movie features, like budget, box office, year, language and so on. The main idea behind this model is that movies belonging to one group must have great similarity.

For those who love independent movies, I partitioned a indie movie list based on their features like budget, review times. If the movies a customer has rated are almost from the indie movie list, the engine would recommend other indie movies which have high ratings and similar features.

Email Digest Analysis

This is a project I conducted for an online media company to to investigate how users interact with their email digest content. The purpose of this project is to analyze the customer page views data in food and recipe to summarize interesting patterns and insights that they can leverage to help improve their email strategy. According to the dataset, I applied my analysis on some basic components such as article contents, campaign channels, and timestamp information.

For the content analysis, I compared the popularity of different article topics and formats, and I also partitioned the customers into different groups according to their browsing preference. In the campaign analysis, I investigated page views that come from different sources which bring the most traffic. Besides, I found out views from different channels are affected by time pattern and article content. Then I implemented another criteria to group customers based on their visit frequency, which means to find out patterns to drive growth in customer engagement.

Based on the analysis, I summarized four data informed recommendations to the marketing team to improve their email digest strategy on content filtering and push frequency.
